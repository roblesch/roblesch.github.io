Rendering

Russian Roulette: Albedo to EARS, USC Directed Research, Spring 2023, advised by Professor Ulrich Neumann. Russian Roulette and Splitting are techniques which can improve the efficiency of an estimator without introducing bias. This project implements a unidirectional forward path tracer demonstrating Rath et. al.'s *EARS* (SIGGRAPH 2022), a learning-based method for the iterative optimization of Russian Roulette & Splitting parameters with respect to estimator efficiency.

MaterialX glTF Export Workflow, Autodesk Internship, Summer 2022. MaterialX is a widely adopted look-development authoring and transport format backed by the Academy Software Foundation. This project extended MaterialX with export from Autodesk Standard Surface to glTF and verifies translation on the AMD GPU Open MaterialX sample set via Arnold and Dassault Systèmes' dspbr-ptr, and was contributed back to MaterialX as open source.

Procedural Materials and Constructive Geometry, USC Graphics & Rendering Final Project, Spring 2022. Fully ray-marched renderer demonstrating the capabilities of signed distance functions with constructive geometry, procedural depth-marched materials, fractals, and GPU Accelerated rendering with CUDA.

Multiple Importance Sampling, Winter 2022. A review of Veach’s thesis and exploration of fundamental techniques in Monte Carlo Integration and Multiple Importance Sampling.

Game Development

Grandma Green, USC Advanced Game Projects, 2022. Developing a virtual pet and farming simulation game in Unity through USC's Advanced Game Projects program. I'm responsible for the design and development of the performant data structures and routines for plant growth cycles, genotype expression, sprite management and garden serialization.

Mixed Media

3D Style Transfer, June 2021. Exploration of visual effects from Aphex Twin's "T69 Collapse" with re-texturing of a scanned 3d mesh in Blender. Extended with exploration of feasibility of real-time style transfer plugin in TouchDesigner.
